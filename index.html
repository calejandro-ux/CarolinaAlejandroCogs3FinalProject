Biostatistics Research Project

For this research project in my Public Health 102 class, we were tasked to analyze and interpret data based on the topic of our choice. Me and my group chose to analyze the effects of caffeine intake on the hours of sleep of UC San Diego Students. We created a questionnaire and sent it out to over 100 students at UC San Diego through group chats, social media, slack, and discord.

After analyzing our data we ​​concluded that there is a significant difference in average sleep duration between UCSD students who consume under 150 mg of caffeine per day and those who consume 150 mg or more. The results suggest that higher daily caffeine intake may negatively impact sleep duration. Specifically, UCSD students who consumed 150 mg or more of caffeine per day slept significantly fewer hours on average than students who consumed less than 150 mg.

This assignment furthered my skills for nursing such as interpreting data, identifying patterns, and considering real-world health implications. As a nurse I will also have to rely on evidence-based practice to understand patient risks, identify trends, and make informed recommendations. This project has helped me understand how everyday habits influence health, strengthening my ability to interpret clinical data, and preparing me to educate patients on improving their well-being.

Gun & IPV Analysis

In this presentation for my Public Health 120 class, we were tasked with researching the effectiveness of different public health policies and evaluating how well they address real-world problems. My group chose to focus on gun and intimate partner violence against women; a major public health issue with serious physical, emotional, and social consequences. We analyzed the policy entitled “Integrated Background Check for Intimate Partner Violence (IPV) Offenders.” This required us to search for peer-reviewed articles, compare study designs, interpret data, and determine whether the policy successfully reduced intimate partner homicides.

Completing this assignment deepened my understanding of how policies directly shape health outcomes and safety in our communities. This experience strengthened my ability to evaluate evidence and think critically. Most importantly, this project helped me recognize the seriousness of the issues I may encounter as a future nurse.
